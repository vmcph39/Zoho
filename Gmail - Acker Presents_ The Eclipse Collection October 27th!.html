Though sizeable in its own right, this consignment of nearly 400 lots is but the very tip of the iceberg of our collector’s mammoth cellar that is well past 40,000 bottles! The quality here is also top of the mark as he has kept extensive records over his two decades of collecting, knowing the provenance of each and every bottle with a rarely seen precision. His collecting strategy was profoundly simple and immensely successful: to buy the best producers, in the best vintages from the best sellers and cellars. Oh, and of course to focus on his favorite region of them all, Burgundy.

Our New York City collector made his way in finance after growing up in New England in a culturally Italian household around the table with family, food, and wine. Although the wine wasn't the focus and not terribly good, it planted the seed for his future vinous pursuits. While dating his then girlfriend, now wife, he wanted to impress her with his wine choices, so he started ardently learning all he could about wine. Wine quickly went from an interest to a passion and eventually the all-encompassing obsession that it is today. He fell in love with Barolo after drinking one for the first time at Babbo and after visiting the region, he then quickly moved onto all the French classics, particularly Burgundy and Champagne. Some of his most memorable “epiphany” bottles from those early days were 1989 Giacosa Barolo Villero, 1988 Krug and 1999 Jamet. 
Those formative experiences of sharing wine around a table with great company is what in many ways has driven his collecting.  His obsession for wine is not for buying or owning it but rather for drinking and enjoying it.  Every bottle in his extensive cellar was meticulously chosen with the intention to enjoy it himself and with fellow wine adorers.  These were not investment pursuits, these were bought and selected with great love, care, and rigorous vetting as they were destined for his personal consumption.  His famous generosity at dinners is a testament to his ethos of sharing great bottles with great company, but as he came to recently admit, he simply has way too much wine.  He also enjoys the thrill of acquiring the newest vintages as they get released, so he cannot stop!  It’s still very difficult for him to sell his meticulously sourced beauties, which is why you will see installments from the collection.  He wanted to sell more, but the process is very painful for him!  All his wine has been primarily bought through trusted local retailers and the major auction houses, stored professionally in multiple locations on the East Coast.
